Upgrading regional after sales service in the cloud era

York by Johnson Controls

After Sales Service quickly get launched on cloud

Service Cloud Agile Development Heroku
Challenge
Global strategy to provide cloud base integrated service needs to be launched with a newly acquired brand. A tight execution pipeline requires regional co-ordination.
Solution
Prioritize and speed up software delivery by Agile development. Weekly testing and reviews take place in Heroku stating environment.
Benefit
Quick nailing down requirements and settle concerns across region. Flexible product that matches global standard and local need.

Johnson Controls Inc. (JCI) is a Fortune 500 company which embraces integrity and ingenuity. Its building efficiency unit is a leading provider of HVAC products, handling a huge customer base. When Johnson Controls acquired York International and entered south east asia market, the need to consolidate scattered customer and support service information was very prominent.

Johnson Controls had already setup a global strategy to ride on Salesforce platform, which is very scalable. However, there were still a lot of work to do in order to adopt practical needs and local considerations, and these works needed to be done quickly.

When JCI HK approached WebFusion, they required a ready to go task force not only knowing Salesforce integration, but also able to adopt the new Heroku Enterprise enviroment, and make every part of the system scalable and flexible. Flexibility here meant different project leaders had different understanding of what the system should do, and WebFusion as development partner had to delivery working product in a rapid update cycle to support their decision continuously.

We went to coach and work immediately. Our Project Manager acted as Agile development coach and made sure task priority matched with the busy correspondants. With intense meetings and weekly staged deliverables, we successfully delivered the online product registration interface within four weeks.

Then we rolled on to sketch out the requirements and design for the rest of the system, with future integration with Salesforce specification in mind. Thanks to the rapid progress enabled by our agile methodology, we went from design wireframes to systems in production, including a custom hosted helpdesk platform, mobile apps, the mobile backend system, and a telephony solution, in just 3 months.

Roger So

Architect

Roger has tinkered with computers since primary school, and first toyed with Linux and open source almost 20 years ago, which has been his passion ever since. He loves trying to squeeze out maximum performance out of minimal hardware, both physical and virtual, ranging from smartphones to private clouds spanning multiple data centres.

He received degrees in Computer Science and Computer Engineering from the University of Melbourne, and has worked with both large enterprises and small startups in Australia and Hong Kong, before joining WebFusion. Roger has also participated in industry organisations, and has chaired the Chinese, Japanese and South Korean governments' standardisation workgroups. He is the co-author of China's GB/T 25656-2010 industry standard.
