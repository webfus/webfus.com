Upgrading regional after sales service to the cloud era

York by Johnson Controls

After Sales Service quickly launched on cloud

Service Cloud Agile Development Heroku
Challenge
Johnson Controls Inc. (JCI) planned to launch a cloud-based integrated service with a newly acquired brand, which went in line with the company’s global strategy. However, the project was constrained by a tight execution timeline and heavy regional coordination.
Solution
Applying agile development methodology, Web Fusion identified priorities in the project, and held weekly testing and reviews with the client in Heroku stating environment, so as to ensure software delivery was smooth and within client’s expectation.
Benefit
Our approach helped the client to align expectations and requirements across its regional offices. The agile development methodology allowed flexibility for the software delivery meeting both global corporate standards and local business needs.

Johnson Controls Inc. (JCI) is a Fortune 500 company and embraces both integrity and ingenuity. Its Building Efficiency unit is a leading provider of HVAC products which earned a huge customer base for the company. When Johnson Controls acquired York International and entered the South East Asia market, the need to consolidate scattered customer and support service systems came to the company’s top priority.

JCI has a global strategy in place to ride on the Salesforce platform given its flexible nature to be scaled. Nevertheless, it took a lot of work to deploy a global strategy in local offices and even more work to make it practical. As usual, all of the above needed to be completed within quickest possible time.

When JCI HK approached Web Fusion, they asked not only for a ready-to-go taskforce of Salesforce integration know-how, but also quick learners who could seamlessly adopt to the new Heroku Enterprise environment, so as to ensure every part of the new system would support further scaling and allow some flexibility. Flexibility here means the system would deliver according to different specifications made by different project leaders. As a development partner, Web Fusion had to deliver a working product with a rapid update cycle to support the project leader’s decision-making process continuously.

Web Fusion’s Project Manager played dual roles: as an agile development coach and facilitator for helping stakeholders to prioritize their needs, immediately after appointment of the project. With intense meetings and weekly staged deliverables, the team successfully delivered the online product registration application within four weeks.

With future integration with Salesforce specification in mind, the requirements and design for the rest of the system were sketched closely after the four week’s period. Thanks to the rapid progress enabled by the agile development methodology, Web Fusion went from design wireframes to systems in production, including a custom hosted helpdesk platform, mobile apps, the mobile backend system, and a telephony solution, within three months.
